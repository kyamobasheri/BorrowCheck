Why most people struggle

The problem with going straight
to a broker

Not all mortgage brokers are the same — and going to the wrong one first can cost you time, and in some cases, affect your credit file.

Brokers aren't all generalists

Some brokers specialise in first-time buyers. Others focus on self-employed applicants or bad credit cases. Going to the wrong one means they may not have access to the right lenders for your situation.

Lenders assess you differently

Different lenders have very different criteria — for income multiples, credit history, employment type, and deposit requirements. Most people don't find out how they'll be viewed until they're already in the process.

Rejections waste time and risk your record

A declined mortgage application can stay on your credit record. Understanding your options first means you only apply when you have a realistic chance — protecting your credit file and saving time.
